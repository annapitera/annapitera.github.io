What Are Terrapins?

Terrapins are one of many small species of turtle. Their name comes from an Algonquian Indian word meaning "a little turtle." This word became popular to describe them during the colonial era of North America. However, what terrapins are called depends on the country you live in; for example, in Austrialia, terrapins can be referred to as tortoises. This often causes confusion along with how similar turtles, tortoises, and terrapins look. Tortoises often spend most of their life on land, while terrapins are semi-aquatic. They typically live in water with a salinity between fresh and marine water, otherwise known as brackish water. However, over time, they have adapted to survive in other salinities. One species of terrapin, the diamondback terrapin, is native to the eastern and southern United States as well as Bermuda. A subspecies of diamondback terrapins are the Northern diamondback terrapins, which can be found in New Jersey.
