Conservation Efforts

Unfortunately, diamondback terrapins are classified as an endangered species. This is a result of many contributing factors, with the main one being humans. Throughout history, terrapins were often used in turtle soup and other dishes. While this practice isn't as common today, it certainly isn't gone completely. Another massive issue is crab traps; terrapins often get stuck in them and die. Because of this, some terrapin populations are unevenly male-dominated. Like many other animals, their habitats are also in great danger. They are also known to be killed by being run over on the road. In addition to all of this, they can also be hunted down for the illegal pet trade. Some people breed terrapins in captivity for the desire of specific color variants. Their population numbers today are still decreasing, but this is being helped by many conservation efforts. Bycatch reduction devices can be placed on crab traps to prevent them from getting stuck. Road patrols during nesting seasons can help avoid road mortality. There have also been many barrier fences put in place in terrapin habitats to prevent them from getting onto roads and into dangerous areas.
