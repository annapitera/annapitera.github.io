Conservation Efforts

Unfortunately, diamondback terrapins are classified as an endangered species. This is a result of many contributing factors, with the main one being humans. Throughout history, terrapins were often used in turtle soup and other dishes. While this practice isn't as common today, it certainly isn't gone completely. Another massive issue is crab traps; terrapins often get stuck in them and die. Because of this, some terrapin populations are unevenly male-dominated. Like many other animals, their habitats are also in great danger.
