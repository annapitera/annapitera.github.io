Habits

Terrapins are generally very social animals, often being seen basking together during the day. They live in the water for most of their life, but often leave to bask and lay eggs. There is some scientific evidence to prove that adult diamondbacks hibernate in the colder months, especially in the mud of creeks and marshes. In the wild, they are quick to flee and difficult to observe. They are great swimmers and will go to water if approached. In captivity, they are known to recognize habits, like what times people are normally around. One helpful adaptation they possess is salt glands around their eyes; these allow them to secrete excess salt from the water they're in.
